Tokyo is a vibrant, magical city that makes you fall in love the moment you land. Being home to around 14 million people, it is one of the largest cities in the world. You can easily find yourself strolling around Tokyo’s diverse neighborhoods and completely lose track of time.

You can spend weeks exploring this bustling city, with numerous day trips and unique cultural experiences. Tokyo offers the ultimate travel package — from wild nightlife and futuristic skyscrapers to serene escapes like peaceful gardens and ancient temples.

While you’re constantly on your feet enjoying the city, **Tokyo's food scene** becomes a highlight in itself. As a Muslim tourist, you’ll be glad to know that there are several halal-certified restaurants across the city. The **NPO Japan Halal Association**, approved by international bodies like JAKIM (Malaysia), MUIS (Singapore), and ESMA (UAE), helps certify Halal eateries across Japan.

Let’s dive in and explore the **top Halal restaurants in Tokyo**:

---

## 1. Halal Ramen Honolu Ebisu

![Halal Ramen Honolu Ebisu](/images/blog/pexels-catscoming-1395319_600x600.webp)

If you're near Ebisu Station and craving a warm bowl of ramen, **Halal Ramen Honolu** is a must-visit. This cozy restaurant specializes in authentic Japanese ramen that’s 100% Halal.

With perfectly seasoned broth, tender meat, and just-the-right-texture noodles, it provides a true ramen experience while keeping everything Muslim-friendly. Quick service and an accommodating staff make it even more worthwhile. The space is small and can get crowded, so be prepared to wait during peak hours.

**Address:** 1-23-1 Ebisu Minami America-Bashi Bldg. 1F, Shibuya 150-0022 Tokyo  
**Phone:** +81 3-5734-1667  
**Hours:** 11:30 AM - 2:30 PM, 5:00 PM - 10:00 PM  
**Website:** [Link](http://halalramen-honolu.net/)  
**Cuisine:** Japanese

---

## 2. A5 Halal Wagyu Yakiniku Panga

![A5 Halal Wagyu Yakiniku Panga](/images/blog/pexels-hasan-kurt-154798938-10749578_600x600s.webp)

Looking for a **premium halal dining experience** in Tokyo? Head to **Yakiniku Panga** in Taito for some of the best A5-grade Halal Wagyu beef in the city.

If you're in Japan, skipping Wagyu would be a miss. This high-end restaurant serves beef that practically melts in your mouth. It’s a small, often fully booked space — so reservations are highly recommended.

A bit on the pricier side, but every bite justifies the cost.

**Address:** 3 Chome-27-9 Taito, Taito City, Tokyo 110-0016, Japan  
**Phone:** +81 3-3839-8929  
**Hours:** 11:30 AM - 10:30 PM  
**Website:** [Link](http://www.wagyu-panga.jp/menu.html)  
**Cuisine:** Japanese, Western

## 3. Luxe Burgers and Sunny’s Table

![Luxe Burgers and Sunny’s Table](/images/blog/pexels-jonathanborba-2983101_600x600.webp)

Craving burgers instead of sushi? **Luxe Burgers and Sunny’s Table** offers delicious Halal fast food — including their famous Wagyu beef burger.

This place blends a stylish ambiance with casual dining and has been rated as one of the **top Halal restaurants** by Halal Gourmet Japan in 2020. Ideal for those who want a quick, satisfying meal with familiar flavors.

**Address:** 6-13 Nihonbashikobunacho, Chuo City, Tokyo 103-0024, Japan  
**Phone:** +81 3-6206-2466  
**Hours:** 11:30 AM - 9:00 PM  
**Website:** [Link](https://luxe-burgers.com/halal/)  
**Cuisine:** Japanese, Fast Food

---

## 4. Annam Indian Restaurant Ginza Tokyo

![Annam Indian Restaurant Ginza Tokyo](/images/blog/pexels-chanwalrus-958545_600x600.webp)

Established in 1968, **Annam** is one of the oldest Indian restaurants in Tokyo. Located just a 5-minute walk from Ginza Station (Exit A3), it offers a variety of North Indian and Pakistani dishes, customized for local tastes.

The restaurant has plenty of seating, including private rooms, counters, and booths. Prices are on the higher side, but it’s well worth the experience.

**Address:** Ginza 8-10 Building, B1F, 8 Chome-10-8 Ginza, Chuo City, Tokyo 104-0061, Japan  
**Phone:** +81 3-6280-6708  
**Hours:** 11:00 AM - 3:00 AM  
**Website:** [Link](https://annam-indian-restaurannt.owst.jp/)  
**Cuisine:** Indian, Pakistani

## 5. Abu Essam Arabic Café and Deli

![Abu Essam Arabic Café and Deli](/images/blog/pexels-shameel-mukkath-3421394-5175623_600x600.webp)

In the heart of Kagurazaka, **Abu Essam Café** brings authentic **Middle Eastern cuisine** to Tokyo. Whether you're craving falafel, shawarma, kebabs, or Arabic sandwiches, you’ll find it here.

They serve lunch and offer dinner by reservation. The atmosphere is homey and the food is thoughtfully prepared with balanced spices and generous portions. Great for dine-in or takeaway.

**Address:** Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo  
**Phone:** +81 3-5946-8014  
**Hours:** 11:00 AM - 3:00 PM, 4:00 PM - 9:00 PM  
**Website:** [Link](https://abuessam.jp/)  
**Cuisine:** Arabic, Middle Eastern

---

## 6. Bharati Indian Restaurant

![Bharati Indian Restaurant](/images/blog/pexels-marvin-ozz-1297854-2474661_1_600x600.webp)

Located just a few steps from Yoyogi Hachiman Station, **Bharati Indian Restaurant** is a cozy spot known for its warm service and flavorful Indian food.

Vegetarian-friendly with a wide menu, it offers classic Indian dishes in a quiet setting. Though slightly expensive, the food quality and hospitality make it a worthwhile visit.

**Address:** City House Yoyogikoen, 2-9 Motoyoyogicho, Shibuya City, Tokyo 151-0062, Japan  
**Phone:** +81 3-3481-6646  
**Hours:** 11:00 AM - 3:00 PM, 5:00 PM - 9:00 PM  
**Social Media:** [Link](https://www.instagram.com/bharati.yoyogi/p/DByWRKXSd2h/)  
**Cuisine:** Vegetarian, Indian
